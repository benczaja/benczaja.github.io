3D Cell Resolved Blood Modeling

I worked on multiple projects developing the high-performance framework HemoCell, which is used for simulating dense cellular suspension flows. My work focused studying the rheology and transport phenomena of whole blood, with specfic applications to diseases like Diabetes and Sickle Cell Anemia.

2D Cell Resolved Blood Modeling

I developed and applied simplified cell resolved blood flow model HemoCell2D. Such simplfied 2D models allows us to study much larger spatial-temporal scales which are unachieaveble by full 3D models. Specifically I developed the model to encorperate a 1D heart beat to simulate systolic flow, and its influence in the transport of red blood cells and platelets into simplified saccular aneurysm geometries.

Heterogenous Multiscale Modelling of Blood Flow

Due to the computational overhead of cell resolved blood flow models, a blood flow model is developed over multiple spatial scales which accurately and efficiantly captures the non-Newtonian rheology of whole blood. Here both continuum and cell resolved blood flow models are employed on multiple spatial scales to simulate blood flow in vessel currently out of reach for contemporary cell resolved blood flow models.
